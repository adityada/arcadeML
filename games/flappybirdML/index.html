How It Works
Neural Network Model

As seen from the demonstration on the left, this particular neural network has five inputs: the y-position of the bird, the top y-position of the closest pipe, the bottom y-position of the closest pipe, and the velocity of the bird. This is a simpler implementation of reinforcement learning as it has access to its environment's variables - while most deep reinforcement agents nowadays use pixels as inputs, like Google's DeepMind StarCraft AI. Furthermore, it has eight hidden layers. This number is arbitrary, a result of countless tinkering and trial-and-error. The output has merely one layer, meaning it decides whether to jump or not.

How it Learned

The learning process involves the use of Genetic Algorithms and Neural Evolution. For a more comprehensive explanation, go to the Train Your Own Flappy Bird AI page.
